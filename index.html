My View on Life

We don't really know if life needs a purpose. Whether someone becomes a billionaire or someone struggles for bread, we all stand equal in front of meaninglessness. Across Earth's species, most don't even know they are alive—self-consciousness feels like a gift and a curse. We are complex chemistry studying itself, a rare randomness in a patterned universe. Maybe life is an error, or a beautiful glitch. Everything forms and dissolves; thoughts loop endlessly. So perhaps life has no built-in meaning—it just starts and ends. By accident we evolved far enough to ask 'why'. I live with that randomness—quietly, curiously.

Purpose of Life

I don't think life arrives with a built-in purpose. The universe runs on patterns and physics, yet somehow we appear—self-aware, restless, and asking questions no one promised to answer. If everything else follows its pattern and dissolves, then maybe meaning isn't found in a single destination but in how we pay attention while we are here.

For me, purpose is a practice:

  • To stay curious when randomness feels heavy.
  • To bring calm where noise is loud.
  • To notice beauty—the quiet tree line, a moving melody, a kind word—and let it change me.
  • To learn with discipline: math, algorithms, programming, and the craft of thinking clearly.
  • To create small, honest things—an idea, a sketch, a paragraph of code—that make reality a little gentler.
  • To play the infinite game: improve the system without needing to "win".

I don't need the universe to guarantee meaning. I choose to make meaning by how I live: by caring, by building simply, by learning continuously, and by letting peace guide me. If all of this is a beautiful glitch, I'll still try to be a good one.
